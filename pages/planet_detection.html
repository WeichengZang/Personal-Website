The Gravitational Microlensing Technique

A microlensing event occurs when the foreground lens and the background source are well aligned. According to General Relativity, the light from the background source is refracted and we can observe a light curve with the change in magnification of the source flux as a function of time. If one of the lensing images passes by or is close to a planet around the lens star, the planet can produce a perturbation on the light curve. In the right panel (credit: ESA), the planet induces a short-lived bump after the main peak.
