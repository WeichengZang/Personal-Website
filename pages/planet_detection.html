The Gravitational Microlensing Technique

If there is a foreground lens star transiting the line of sight, due to the general relativity, the light of the background source star will be refracted and then we can receive more flux from the source star. The typical angular Einstein radius of microlensing events is ~0.5 mas, so now it is almost impossible to resolve the microlensing images, and what we observe is a symmetric, non-repeating, and single-peak light curve. If one of the lensing images passes by or is close to a planet around the lens star, the planet can produce a perturbation on the light curve.
